## Final staggered mesh solution

Some grids have:

- 141 radii (29.257338 to 237.20267) vs. 140 radii (30. to 236.46) - uniform steps of 1.485323
    - in 141 radii mesh, r starts half a step lower (30 - 29.257338 = 1.485323 / 2) and ends half a step higher (237.20267 - 236.46 = 1.485323 / 2)
- 111 theta (-0.014410975389182568 to 3.156003713607788) vs. 110 theta (0 to 3.1415927410125732)
    - similar to the radial situation, half a step closer and half a step further

Solution

The final grid is the largest grid but ignoring the first and last coordinates, (e.g., 141 becomes 139, 111 becomes 109). And the smaller grids (originally 140 radii or 100 theta) get linearly interpolated into 139 and 109 as well. For example for $$v_r$$ with shape (140, 111, 128) radial interpolation we'll do `vr_center = 0.5 * (vr_face[..., 1:] + vr_face[..., :-1])`

Thus the common grid in MAS is (139, 109, 128).
